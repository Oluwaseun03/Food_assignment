Jollof rice is a West African dish that’s a staple in most households. It definitely was a staple in my house growing up, and at parties, where you can be rest assured that there will be pans and pans of it. This is the first reason I love attending parties. There many ways to cook Jollof Rice. It is a dish made with rice tomato paste onions red bell pepper scotch bonnet pepper salt and spices it is also made with so much love.

Preparation time: 20mins

Cook time: 40mins

Serves: 5 people

Ingredients Measurement
Tomatoes 5 medium sized
Red bell pepper 1 medium sized
Onions 1 medium sized(chopped)
Scotch bonnet pepper 2 medium sized
Groundnut oil ¼ cup
Tomato paste 3 tablespoon
Parboiled rice 2 cups
Chicken stock 2 cups
Salt To taste
Curry powder ½ tablespoon
Thyme ½ tablespoon
Knorr cubes 1 cube
Bay leaves 3 leaves
Water As needed

Directions

  1. Blend your tomatoes, red pepper, scotch bonnet peppers in a food processor or blender for about 45 seconds, make sure that everything is well blended
  2. In a medium sized pot, heat your oil on medium-high heat. Once the oil is heated add your onions you set aside and fry just until they turn golden brown. Once the onions have turn brown in color add the tomato paste and fry for 2-3 minutes. Then add the blended tomato mixture and fry for about 30 minutes. Make sure you stir consistently so that the tomato mixture does not burn.
  3. After 30 minutes turn down the heat to medium and add the chicken stock. Mix and add all your seasonings (salt, curry powder, thyme, and the Knorr cubes). Continue to cook for 10 minutes.
  4. Add the parboiled rice to the pot. Mix it very well with the tomato stew.at this point if you need to add water so that the rice is level with the tomato/chicken stock go ahead and do so. Add the bay leaves, cover pot, and cook on medium to low heat for about15-30 minutes.
